Ink = .25[4]

Mailer = .75[5]

Postage = 1.75[6]

Domestic Retail Additions:

Labor and Travel = 2.00[7]

Computer Fund = 1.00[8]

Free CD Fund = 1.00[9]

4.00

 

International Materials Cost:

CD = .50

Jewel Case =.75

Cover and Label = .25

Ink = .25

Mailer = 1.50[10]

Postage = 3.50[11]

6.75 to produce and mail each CD

 

International Retail Additions:

4.50

 

 


[1] I buy my blank CD's by the stack of 100.  The price varies between $25 and $35 per 100, plus tax, etc.

[2] I buy these by the stack of 50 for about $25 to $35 also.

[3] I print my own covers on good quality 110lb white stock paper ($12 a ream) and I print (and "stomp") my own labels ($20 per 100)

[4] Oy!  I have one of those inkjet printers that takes expensive ink cartridges!  One tri-color cartridge runs $30, plus tax.

[5] This is an average cost between the less expensive mailers for just one or two CD's and the more expensive boxes necessary for larger orders.

[6] To mail a single CD in the most lightweight mailer that I have, costs around $2.10, so this figure is averaged down to $1.75 to compensate for multiple CD orders.

[7] This is a nominal charge for the time it takes me to physically produce each CD and to drive to the post office to mail it.

[8] Each CD is "burned" on my own home computer and each label and jewel case cover is printed on my home printer.  This $1.00 helps me to pay off the purchase of this computer and see to its upkeep.

[9] :)  Each person who purchases a CD also helps me afford to offer free CD's to those who can't afford the purchase price.

[10] I have to use sturdier mailers for international orders, hence the increase over the Domestic cost.

[11] Heavier mailers and overseas postal rates (Air Mail) mean greater costs.

[12] I'm charging $.50 more because I hate having to fill out those pesky customs declarations!  ;-)
