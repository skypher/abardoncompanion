Dear Mark,

In mathematics, infinity can varying degrees of infiniteness. This 
can easily be proven using power set composition. If you want me to 
explain further I will. Basically, you can have an infinity that is 
greater than another infinity. This sort of happens in physics with 
cosmology. They've pretty much proven mathematically that the 
universe is spherical and will not collapse in on itself. There are 
two other possibilities: everything keeps moving away, or we 
approach a limit. With the former, we would eventually be left all 
alone in what we could see in the universe. In the latter case, we 
would appear to drift so far apart and then come to a halt, but not 
quite. We still drift further apart, just at a slower rate. 
Varying degrees of infinity ;)

Not sure if this helps, but it may lead to some new angles with 
which to view your question.
