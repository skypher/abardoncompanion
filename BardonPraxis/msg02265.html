Dear Rawn,

>>Perhaps this can teach you something about the nature of one-
pointedness and the connection between interest/significance and 
mental focus?<<

In fact, it was more or less one of my recent one pointedness 
topics:-) 
In Step I Bardon says that we need to concentrate on whatever we do 
in our everyday lives without letting unrelated thoughts cross our 
minds. So I was thinking, "How can I really achieve this goal?" 
Briefly, I came to conclusion that since there is a strong positive 
corelation between mental focus and signifcance/interest, we should 
try to "make" our every action significant to us. We should not 
understimate our actions, even if this is something that we do every 
day. If we manage to do so than maintaining mental focus will be 
easier. And of course, it will also make life more interesting.

Thanks,
David
