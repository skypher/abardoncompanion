The new section I introduce is the availability of .PDF versions of
several of Bardon's books, in various languages!

For several years now, .PDF files of many of Bardon's books have been
available on the Internet for anybody to download - if you knew where to
look. Recently, several of these files were posted together on the
BardonPraxis discussion group's website by different members.
Unfortunately access to this resource is limited to only those who join
that group, so I decided to house them on ABardonCompanion.com as
well.

Historically, I've expressed my concerns about the copyright questions
that the existence of these .pdf versions raises. But since there has
been no action, nor any public statement of displeasure, by the
copyright holders in response to their existence all these years, I've
finally decided to forego my own concerns and see to it that they are at
least gathered together in one central location for convenience sake.

As a writer of books myself and thus a person somewhat involved in the
publishing world, I am sensitive to copyright violation and the
significant impact that such violations have upon the financial
viability of a publishing business. However, I'm also very sensitive to
the economic struggles we all face in this day - a struggle that
prevents many from affording the considerable expense of purchasing
Bardon's books. It has not been an easy decision for me to post these
.PDF versions of Bardon's copyrighted works, but what sways me is my
interest in seeing that Bardon's work remain available *even to those
who cannot afford the price of his books*. In consideration for my own
struggle with this issue I ask that, if you do use these files and like
what they contain, you start saving your money to purchase them in book
form. :)

So now they can be found on my PDF-Links page
http://www.ABardonCompanion.com/PDF-Links.html
where I also offer downloadable PDF versions of my own work. There you
will find: "IIH" in 4 languages (English, German, Portuguese and
Romanian [kindly donated by the translator]); "PME" in German; "KTQ" in
English and German; "Frabato" in German; and, "Souvenirs" in German.

Have a joyous Valentine's Day!
