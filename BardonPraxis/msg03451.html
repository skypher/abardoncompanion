After hearing about a Quantum Physics "law" called non-locality (and 
I would summarise it here, but I'd end up re-telling the experiment 
wrongly), it got me thinking about how it may apply to some of 
Bardon's exercises.

For example, if I were to drink half a glass of water and then 
impregnate the undrunk half with an ideation, would the Akasha of 
the water I had already drunk take on the same ideation? And if this 
did apply, how would it apply to food? Would the food have to have 
been part of a whole (e.g. eat half of a burger and then impregnate 
the other half) or could the food just have a sympathetic link, for 
example chips which were cooked together or which came from the same 
potato?

Hmmm, I suppose then we enter the realm of the Mind and an 
individual's perception of what constitutes a whole.... a reality 
created through the thoughts of an individual. OK, then that would 
possibly depend on the abilities and mental focus and Will of that 
person.

Now I guess this is also linked to correspondences and using an 
object/item/concept etc to represent something else.

But then everything is linked via the Akasha (as I understand it) 
anyway and so I have come full circle.

So technically *everything* we affect/impregnate/alter has some sort 
of effect on *everything* else.

Help?
