Full moons, including lunar eclipses, are supposed to be a source of
energy, particularly for magical purposes performed by a group of
people. During them, the Moon passes through the "tail" of Earth's
magnetic field, the shape of which is distorted by the "solar wind" of
charged particles (making it compressed toward Earth's surface of the
sunward side, and enlarged on the side facing away from the Sun).
However, in this case, the adverse aspects of other planets to the Sun
and Moon would have result in the "energizing" effects liable to be bad,
with Saturn being square the Sun and Moon, and Mars widely conjunct Sun
and opposite Moon.
