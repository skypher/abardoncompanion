My sincere thanks to all of you who wrote in during this final month of
BardonPraxis with your kind words of appreciation. :) Your sentiments
touched me deeply.

About a dozen years ago I came across the following "Manifesto for the
Teacher" which I have tried my best to keep as a constant guide in all
of my online work. Its simple wisdom has shaped my own definition of my
role as "companion" and of the true companionship which I have tried my
best to offer all of you here. I share it with you now in the hopes
that it will similarly shape how folks who choose to answer questions
interact with those who ask questions, and that it will serve as a
standard of behavior that those who ask questions will hold their
responders to and judge them by.

MANIFESTO FOR THE TEACHER
*Share with us your love -- Do not tell us how to share ours.
*Share with us your wisdom -- Do not speak nor decide for us.
*Share with us your mind -- Do not implant it in us nor tell us what to
do.
*Share with us your light -- Do not tell us where we should spread ours.
*Share with us your power of action -- Do not control this power for us.
*Show us how to spread our wings -- Do not ever clip them.
*Teach us how to learn -- Learn from us how to teach.
