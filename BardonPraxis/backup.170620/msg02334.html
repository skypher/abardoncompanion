Between the mental exercises of step 1 and the mental exercises of step 2 there are two major actions of the mind that need to be brought under control - the mind chatter and the image stream. For the exercises in step 1, Bardon is referring to the semantic mind chatter. Basic success in step 1 is achieved when one is able to observe the mind chatter, limit the mind chatter to a single semantic idea, and finally stop the chatter entirely. However, as one gains proficiency in stemming the mind chatter, the underlying, deeper, image stream becomes more and more apparent. I believe that Bardon left it to the individual to deduce that the same 3 part exercises which worked to stop the mind chatter should then be used to control the image stream. It is only after both of these are brought under control that one can expect to have success with step 2. There may also be a corresponding "chatter" for sense of smell, taste, ect., but I haven't come across them yet.
