Dear 'Metaphysics',

>> I think I've discovered the full 'Golden Book of Wisdom'. According
to several sights I've read, the fragment contained in Frabato was not
infact written by Bardon, hence the different writing style - it was
instead a fragment of a manuscript called the Seven Hermetic Letter
which has been translated by Merkur. <<

There are TWO "fragments" at the end of the English version of Frabato.
The first is "The Golden Book of Wisdom" which was indeed written by
Bardon and is all that remains of his 4th book.

The *second* "fragment" however, is titled "High Magic" and was *NOT*
written by Bardon. When Dieter Ruggeberg first included this in
"Frabato", he assumed it was written by Bardon and labeled it as such.
In the years since then, it's been proven instead to be Bardon's
translation of "Seven Hermetic Letters" which was written by Georg
Lomer.

"The Golden Book of Wisdom" and "Seven Hermetic Letters/High Magic" are
two entirely different works.
