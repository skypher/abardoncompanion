It's very possible that theories from Freud or Jung *could* be very 
helpful for introspection (maybe as a second step); on the other 
hand, they might also make the whole thing more complicate - as Rawn 
once said, you need not read any book, just listen to what the *inner 
voice* is saying, where the root cause or the "why" has to be found 
and solved.

In his answer to Paul Allen, Rawn also mentions, that a magical 
approach may be (or is) different or opposite to a psychological 
approach. This seems very interesting to me!

So my question, where do think, from your experience, did you found 
psychoanalytical (or pyschological) ways to be consistent and 
helpful, and where do you think them to be opposite to a mere magical 
approach as required by Bardons IIH?
