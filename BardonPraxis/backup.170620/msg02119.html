Dear Robert,

>> There are many possibilities when dealing with spiritual occurrences,
but mostly the average person is relatively ignorant of spiritual
phenomena and does not understand what is going on or much less how to
deal with it. IIH does well at revealing the basics of this and putting
it in perspective. <<

It is the *experience of* IIH that leads to understanding, not just the
reading of it. Understanding is not an intellectual process of putting
two and two together. Instead, understanding is the result of direct,
structured personal experience. It is a matter of knowing, not one of
guessing.

An "understanding" that is rooted in what one reads or what one is told
by others is not true Understanding. Instead, that is rationalization,
assumption and belief. From an initiatory perspective, these are not
enough because they are essentially unreliable.
