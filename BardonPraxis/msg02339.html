Hi Jonathan, 

I have done this eyebright bath and found it to work quite well. You make it 
into a tea suffusion and then add it to pure water in a bowl and immerse the 
eyes as Bardon instructs. The tea is strained in a cheesecloth. One can also 
make a bag with the chessecloth wrapped with string and this saves straining. 
Another way is to soak the concoction with cotton balls and apply to the closed 
eyes while restfully lying down. 

I have also use the eyebright tincture made with alcohol which is only used in 
a pure water drink (a few drops in a glass).

Doing this immediately before the exercises is most beneficial.

I became conscious of the plant spirits helping and being tranferred to me 
through their vibrational imprint in these concoctions
