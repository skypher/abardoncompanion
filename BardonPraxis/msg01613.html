I did it that way just to confuse *you*! ;-) ;-) ;-)

Just kidding, of course. There's a specific reason. Have you ever seen
a natal astrological horoscope? The signs are placed in
counter-clockwise order because the perspective is geo-centric. In
other words, the *sky* moves, not the observer. If you stare at the
eastern horizon, the Signs rise and move clockwise across the heavens --
the horizon/observer though, remains stationary.

Since much of the work to be done in the exploration of those Temple
passageways is about the *Individual's* past incarnations, I used the
geo-centric perspective.
