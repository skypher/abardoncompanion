Dear Scott,

>From my experiences with essential meaning, when one percieves 
essential meaning, there is no real *core* to is as there where. 
Essential meaning reflects the totality of the thing that is being 
percieved. As such, you have the perception of dimension, colour, 
history, and so forth permeating that essential meaning. In other 
words, essential meaning seems to consist of a near infinite set of 
datum that, when it is percieved as a whole, can only be done so 
intuitivly with the right side of the brain.

What Rawn calls a voice or a personality of essential meaning, is 
more akin to a finite set of datum that is taken from the totality of 
essential meaning. Generally speaking, this *voice* is something that 
is percieved logically with the left side of the brain. Moreover, 
there are a number of voices that can be derived from essential 
meaning. Some of which speak louder than others.

Love and Live well,
