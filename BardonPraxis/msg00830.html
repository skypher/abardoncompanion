Dear Rawn and Peter,

Thankyou for your replies they helped my understanding immensely.

It is interesting that you brought up exactly what I had intended to say myself 
after reading both your posts. It helps so much to have more than one viewpoint 
or an answer put in a different context.

I also agree that people learn in different ways, some people find it easier to 
reference knowledge, or in this case Hermetic principles to something else in 
order to get a better grasp of what they are studying. 

Others concentrate on the specific pursuit and focus entirely upon that. And I 
guess there is no right way or wrong way just different ways. 

Ever since starting IIH I have put away all other books, beliefs, everything 
not directly related or in some way relevent to the study of Hermetics and 
Bardons material. 

I am one of those people who have to concentrate solely upon the task or 
pursuit at hand to get the most out of it. I find that if there are too many 
cross currents of thoughts and beliefs all happening at once or all trying to 
be reconciled that it dilutes the focus, can cause confusion, and creates a 
scattering of effort. 

Others probably understand easier by being able to draw comparisons between 
different systems and relating them. 

It is very helpful and interesting to hear about peoples experiences put in 
their own unique way.

Kind Regards,

Scott.
