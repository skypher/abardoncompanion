Dear Peter,

>> Within The Practice of Magical Evocation Bardon talks about how upon
the death of a magician his ritual tools should be destroyed so that
they will not be improperly used by another person. <<

An alternative open to a more "advanced" magician is to de-charge one's
own magical tools either before or immediately after one's death.

>> Yet with the magician that has gone through the curriculum of IIH and
KTQ their ritual tools is their own body, in particular their own organs
which they have acclimitated and transformed to various sorts of
energies. Namely, vital energy, the elements, the fluids and the
letters. Something that fundementally alters the structure of the organs
at a very deep level. Now, my question is: should the magician allow his
organs to be transplanted into another person's body upon his death?
And, if so, what would be the possible side effects for the person that
has a magicians' organs transplanted into his own body? <<

The alteration to the physical structure of the organs is negligible.
The primary changes wrought by the practices you cite occur at the
energetic, astral and mental levels. Upon death, the astral and mental
energies exit the physical and energetic materia of the organs. In
other words, the physical organs don't act like a Fluid Condenser which
holds onto the energies after death. This is why donated organs have to
be artificially kept alive.

So I doubt there'd be much difference for the recipient in comparison to
receiving any other person's organ. No matter who the organ comes from
it will be a foreign object which conforms to a foreign astral template
instead of the recipient's own astral template. And even though the
donated organ loses it's own astral template upon the donor's death, the
recipient still faces the task of integrating the physical and energetic
organ into their own astral template and energetic body.

My best to you,
