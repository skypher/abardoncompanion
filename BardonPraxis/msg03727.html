<tt>
Dear 'aha3996',<BR>
<BR>
>> In my reading over the years, many authors insist that one must have<BR>
a personal teacher/"guru" in order to progress along the path. Others<BR>
advocate that one needs to be linked to an order, via initiation, that<BR>
has a lineage back to the original source of the mysteries. Rene Guenon<BR>
advocates this position and then proceeds to tell the reader that at<BR>
the present he knows of no genuine initiatory groups other than the<BR>
shadow of them found in various existing groups of freemasonry. Franz<BR>
Bardon seems to take the opposite view of not being bound by any oath or<BR>
to any group or person with the alternative of following the path on<BR>
your own. Any input out there on these divergent opinions? <<<BR>
<BR>
There are several factors here, foremost of which is that these authors<BR>
were likely taught by others to think that way.  Other factors are that<BR>
they were/are likely involved in a group, involved with a specific<BR>
teacher/guru or are themselves looking for students and thus have a<BR>
vested self-interest in others thinking as they do that a<BR>
teacher/guru/group is *required*.<BR>
<BR>
Also, there are traditions and lineages of practice and dogma that do<BR>
indeed *require* a personal teacher to learn since they are passed along<BR>
only in that way or are so complex that they are impossible to learn on<BR>
one's own.<BR>
<BR>
Bardon's statements about obligating oneself by oath to a teacher or<BR>
group came from his own personal experience of groups and teachers.  His<BR>
point however had little to do with dogmatic tradition but rather,<BR>
pertained specifically to the essential *process* of Hermetic initiation<BR>
itself, stripped of all self-interest and dogma.  The system he<BR>
presented negates the prior need for such obligations because it<BR>
encapsulates the essential work of initiation that, teacher/group or<BR>
not, the student themselves must perform for themselves and on their<BR>
own.  The work he described does not *require* a teacher or group<BR>
because it is something no teacher or group can do for you  --  it's the<BR>
work *you* have to do yourself whether or not a teacher/group is in any<BR>
way involved.<BR>
<BR>
And there it is, all written down and it costs nothing more than the<BR>
price of the book.  And, no oaths or allegiances are required!  :)  The<BR>
only thing required is your own effort . . .<BR>
<BR>
My best to you,<BR>
   :) Rawn Clark<BR>
