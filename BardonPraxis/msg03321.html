Well yeah! You said it right there... I'll try to be more concrete...

Suppose I were very eager and enthusiastic to communicate something to you. 
(Eager/enthusiastic=fire, *Positive* fire!). Then suppose I tried to 
communicate it over and over again, but you didn't get it and I got frustrated, 
angry, and finally flew at you with a pair of daggers and squelched your 
eyeballs. 
Positive fire turning to negative fire! It's that simple, I mean there's 
nothing esoteric about it. At that point if I have no water to fall back on I 
lose my rag. Luckily for us all I have neither your address nor a lack of 
patience (water) and thus am not angry at all! (So please please don't worry 
about 
that!)

So you find there must be a balance. Then you wonder why. Then you think, 
this is supposed to work for everything. Take a look around! In your private 
meditations of the Thought Discipline kind, you can think about *why* fire and 
water might balance each other. It's no good asking me for my experiences 
'cause they have to be yours. That's like asking for my soul mirror man, it 
isn't 
done! You can go out and use your life as a playground to see if you're 
right, by remaining aware during the day - that's the other half of thought 
discipline, and all it means to me is 'try to be the kind of person upon whom 
nothing is lost'. (That's a quote but I can't remember the author, sorry!).

BTW I'm a writer too and you can analyse stories with the Tetragrammaton as 
a key also. If you want to know people's experiences there's alot of books to 
go with. I mean start with Shakespeare! He's good because he wrote about 
nothing but love, but yet there's no happy marriage in Shakespeare (unless you 
are cynical and count the Macbeths!). So why? I mean every real work of art is 
like a soul mirror, you can practice on other people's mirrors that. You can 
come up with a picture of what he was like, what his characters were like... 
it always works.

Then there's air and earth to think about. They balance each other too, but 
there's that tricky thing of seeing why earth is also said to be made of the 
interaction between the other 3 elements. I mean, what *are* all these funny 
old dudes like Franz and Rawn going on about? But you know they know way more 
than you so why not try to see how it applies? Again you can see it 
happening when you stop to think about it, but obviously it has to be you doing 
the 
thinking! Don't worry, it'll all start to clear if you just hammer at it every 
now and then. I wasn't really talking about experiences unique to myself, we 
all have 'em, that's why they say the key is universal! I was trying to pick 
stuff anyone could relate to.


One thing I would state, which Bardon didn't although I believe he might 
have agreed, and that is that: Akasha is love. The other four elements could be 
said also to be just love, or maybe 'the ways in which love happens'. I also 
think that the negative side is best said as fear of love rather than hate. 
Just to throw that in there.

I mean you don't need any more from me! If I can do this anyone can, and 
that's a fact. Just go back to step 1, that's all I've been using - plus 
astrology, psychology, writing, music, I Ching, Enneagram, whatever, but the 
theory 
section of IIH + Step 1 is the thing that makes all the other stuff make 
sense for me, it's the beginning and end, the royal road, it just has obvious 
righteousness. I don't worry these days if I never will get to step 2, I mean 
it! The point is not literally what step you're on, it's just to feel you're 
actually *getting* somewhere. You can be damn sure I'm not leaving step 1 my 
whole life anyhow, but I'm not even touching step 2 until I know there's no way 
I could possibly not have intrinsically completely mastered the whole shit 
out of step 1, pardon my language but there it is. I don't want to wobble 
through on training wheels here!
