Guide for Autumn 2003

Dear Praxis,

Here is my second guide dog for your practice. I hope the first one was
of use to you, and that I will be able to continue to write these
letters to you all in the future. This season I have included a few
solar aspects to show how these colour specific days. I am here to
discuss any of these things with those of you who are curious about
them.

The peak of this autumns planetary transits clusters around the end of
October and beginning of November and involves Saturn and Chiron.

Wishing you a magical time,

Everloving,
Dave

Report Period: 23 Sep 2003 to 22 Dec 2003

Positions of the outer planets, Vernal Equinox – Winter Solstice 2003

Jupiter: 5-18 degrees Virgo
Saturn: 12-13-10 Cancer (stations on 26th Oct at 13 degrees, then turns
retrograde)
Uranus: 29-28-29 degrees of Aquarius (station direct on Nov 9th)
Neptune: 10-11 degrees of Aquarius (station direct Oct 22nd)
Pluto: 17-20 degrees of Sagittarius
Chiron: 12-17 degrees of Capricorn
Head of Draco: 21-19 Taurus (direct Sept 30th – Oct 3rd, then Oct
14th –19th, 27th – Nov 2nd, November 10th – 13th, 18th – 24th, and
December 1st – 7th, 17th – 21st.

Lets look at these positions first.

Jupiter is moving through Virgo with purpose, bringing many great
matters to fruition. This is also characteristic of great cleansings,
purges and the tidying of a big mess. Much of our belief is in simple
and pure things, but we may tend to be blind to a greater picture or
inclined to constipated over analysis. We may need to clear out old
stuff, put things in a new and improved arrangement or order, or tackle
an ever increasing number of seemingly very important things to do in
the day. Yes I know, what the jiminy is new there? ;-) But I think
during this period, we will all be called much more into physical acts
of daily service. Thus, we may need to concentrate on our physical
health, now being an excellent time to start a more nutritious form of
diet. Jupiter is fast approaching a very sharp aspect with Pluto at the
end of the period, which is the crunch of the purification initiated by
Jupiter this season; there will likely be a global increase of tension
(and scandal) at the end of the year, and religion is almost certain to
be at the heart of it. Be aware, however, that this may not necessarily
have direct consequences until the beginning of August 2004, when the
aspect is complete. This aspect also causes conflict in foreign
relations to show itself. In essence, what is happening on both a
collective and individual level is that as we grow more and more
analytical and critical during the purification symbolised by Jupiter,
and then we hit a snare represented by Pluto, which causes us to either
release an ideal or belief, or to revise our plans, and reorganise our
methods and forecasts.

Saturn is hovering over the first three degrees of the second decanate
of Cancer, with an intensive concentration of its energy occurring at
the end of October, around Samhain (Oct 31st). He turns retrograde just
before then; in the sign of Cancer, that of ancestry and memory, family
and heritage, therefore, we can expect the spirits of our ancestors to
draw very close, particularly our elders, grandparents and great
grandparents. Ancient understanding is our teacher now, but we must not
linger in the past, weighed down in memories and nostalgia; instead, we
must restructure what we think of as the notion of family itself, and
Uranus shows us where that boundary must be moved to.

Uranus has as I mentioned last season re-entered Aquarius therefore you
should refer yourselves back to the article I wrote for this summer.
This sign is that of the fellowship of humanity. It is revisiting
Aquarius for the last time now, giving us a last collective opportunity
to envision together and individually our most free future as part of
humanity. Note however that this season its emphasis is on 28 degrees of
Aquarius, (Bardons 29th, the Earth Zone Intelligence named as ‘Nelion’
in PME). The end of October is a time of potential realisation which
leads to awakening. This nascent human awareness of humanity itself
coalesces as a vision through Neptune in Aquarius. As Neptune continues
to sift through the shards of the broken collective human psyche – and
make no doubt, it IS shattered – it will dissolve fragmented ideals back
into themselves and one another, causing a bleed from one ideology and
ideal into the next, rather like a kaleidoscope does with coloured
beads. Consequently, a vision of our future is emerging in our minds eye
– do we like it? Before we can say, it changes! Do we still like it? And
so on. The pattern here only really becomes clear when you take a much
broader and higher perspective – we are being asked to trust and
unconditionally love the rapid and unexpected unpredictable cycling of
things. Most importantly we must rid ourselves of our delusions and
fantasies about the future, for if we do not, they may entrance us while
the opportunity to act at a crucial time slips by.

Pluto continues its crusade through Sagittarius. We have seen more than
the usual level of disturbance related to religious fanaticism, dogma
and cultural ideology bordering on (and sometimes firmly entrenched in)
xenophobia since it inhabited this region. Expect this to be a theme for
some years. Be aware of its more extreme and irrational manifestations
wherever and whenever you encounter them. We counteract the mass
destructive power of Pluto by dedicating ourselves individually to a
transformative process, in this case, one which transmutes our negative
beliefs and the destructive power of extreme faith. Each of us has a
kind of death wish. There is a sense that, these days, there are even
many people who wish we would all just obliterate ourselves, so they can
watch it on TV. We must face this head on. It is born out of a sense of
hopelessness, of a journey too far over the horizon to ever be
completed.

If you have been following politics recently you cant have failed to
notice the influence of teeny tiny Chiron, in Capricorn. Splits,
divisions, crutches, splints and catastrophic weaknesses in government
structure are all under the spotlight. The situation we have as
individuals towards our ambitions reflects this process too. We are
experiencing our own career woundedness, lack of direction, planning, or
foresight in many cases. Some are building bridges between one career
and another. Others are healing through the highest levels of authority
and control. Qualities we may lack that cause pain for us now are self
discipline, scepticism and realism, conservatism, authority and
ambition. If we possess these qualities in excess, they will start to
show cracks, like the very institutions symbolised by Capricorn. This
passage of Chiron is a hand stretched out in help, offering to heal
before Pluto arrives in Capricorn, where it will inevitably deal with
any corruption in its own fashion.

The Head of the Dragon begins the season retrograde but makes no less
than 6 direct turns and 6 retrograde ones before the Winter Solstice,
yet only traversing 3 degrees of Taurus. It is therefore slowed in
comparison to the summer, during which it took 6 direct turns and 6
retrograde and moved 8 degrees. Therefore things will narrow down, less
will happen in terms of society and the interaction of individuals in
groups, so the changes will be more focused. The occult pattern of this
process is hinted at by the three sabian symbols involved. I have given
these in the text below.

Now on with the more specific explanations:

TRANSITING SUN (Virgo) QUINCUNX TRANSITING URANUS (Aquarius)
Entering 22 Sep 2003, Exact 23 Sep 2003, Leaving 24 Sep 2003
This is the last aspect the Sun makes in Virgo before the equinox resets
the clock, so to speak, by initiating a new phase of the total cycle.
The awkward quincunx relationship between Virgo and Aquarius is easy
enough to perceive, but nowhere near as easy to counteract. Its easier
to give you a metaphor drawn from the sabian symbols. Imagine for a
moment you are taking your usual evening stroll somewhere in a place you
have been hundreds of times, but suddenly you stumble on the ruins of
Babylon, and fall into white moonlit fields.

This quincunx grinds together the bizarre and the unexpected with the
logical and day to day practical realities of life, producing an oops.
If this is not experienced externally it often manifests as frustration,
since the 2 forces create friction within us, but only for a day or two.
We keep changing direction because we encounter unforeseen events.
What is required here is a careful recombining of our most rational and
irrational (or eccentric) aspects, so that one does not trip up the
other. Furthermore, we must not let the dizzy trends in society disrupt
our methods or plans, and vice versa! Confusion, accidental occurrence,
irritable and irrational behaviour may surface if we cannot.

TRANSITING SUN ENTERING LIBRA
23 Sep 2003
This is the equinox. It is the point of solar balance and equilibrium, a
magical moment of pause while the momentum shifts from one side of the
cosmic scales to the other. Day and night is balanced. Here the cycle
shifts its focus from development of the personal self, to the dynamics
of interaction with other selves. Thus, in nature, where it signifies a
shift from light to dark, and in human experience, where it signifies a
gradual withdrawal of life energy from the years personal activities,
this point is a place of power, which is always a place where energy
shifts, and the shift in this case is towards our urge to link up with
partners. This is a good time to look for someone if you are lonely, and
to spend time with your loved ones. We must never forget in the course
of initiation that from time to time we have to look up from our
introspection to smell the roses, that is, no human is an island, we all
need each other, and part of learning to be a complete human being is in
relating to everything human equally. Now is the time to focus on doing
that.

TRANSITING SATURN (Cancer) OPPOSITION TRANSITING CHIRON (Capricorn)
In Orb 22 Sep 2003, Exact 25 Sep 2003, Exact 24 Oct 2003, Leaving 3 Dec
2003
This aspect was discussed in the summer report, so again I ask you to
refer yourself back there.

Those of us with yearnings for the past, family, or a home may be
feeling especially vulnerable in these periods. The issue of
homelessness and societies attitude to it is also of significance to
this transit.

Essentially, this aspect gives us an opportunity to recognise our fear
projections and how they are at cross purposes with our memories and
ambitions, distorting past reflections and future objectives. The
Cancer/Capricorn axis is esoterically rooted in the archetypes of the
Mother and the Father, the Nurturer and Provider, and with the
Saturn-Chiron line up in this area, we can expect flare ups against
authority figures in families with teenagers, cracks in the wall of
governing authority, and an extraordinary level of anxiety and
projection in politics.

On an individual level we may feel that our limitations are actually
crippling, that the weight of responsibility is too great, and that our
fear is just too overwhelming, but if we can see through the illusory
nature of material reality we will perceive that there is always pain in
birth. If the umbilicus connecting us to a past environment is holding
us back, these are the times it may be severed.

Whatever blocks or obstacles appear in our path at these times, the
direct motion of Mars as it slides through Pisces towards Aries should
help us face them. Mars turns direct on Sept 27th, which means that from
that day, we steadily become more active and assertive, our expressions
of Mars grow more external, and he grows in power until he enters Aries
in December, whereupon his most forceful face shows itself. As he
journeys through Pisces, a series of encounters with the other planets
describe his moods. In the beginning they are constructive (trine Saturn
in Cancer, 14th Nov) and healing (sextile Chiron in Capricorn 18th Nov),
but later they become too fussy, dogmatic and idealistic (opposition to
Jupiter in Virgo, Nov 20th) and later still explosive and potentially
self destructive (square Pluto in Sagittarius, Nov 26th), Mars finally
adopting his most warlike and confrontational aspect in Aries (as of Dec
16th). This period, then, of Mars in Pisces, is the calm before the
storm, my friends. The beginning of next year, Mars opens January 1st
with a square to Saturn in Cancer. I will have more to say of this in
the winter solstice report. For now, bear in kind that this is a time to
muster your energy and gather the power of emotional sensitivity to the
suffering of others.

limited to the end of Oct. It is advisable that if you must have
important dealings with authorities of any kind, you try and do so
before this period begins, and the sooner the better!

consciousness and awareness becomes overwhelmingly emotional (Uranus in
Pisces).

TRANSITING JUPITER (Virgo) SQUARE TRANSITING PLUTO (Sagittarius)

Jupiter is the planetary force of growth and expansion; as it travels

to find the right balance of confidence and patience.

Jupiter is also linked with faith and belief and at this time our faith
may be severely tested. If it is like an old rope, frayed and weak, our
faith may break, and we will then fall; but if our faith is strong and
does not falter, we will overcome many great obstacles. This is a time
in which we can express great spiritual zeal. Spiritual zeal is a great
virtue, for it ensures that we will continue to walk in harmony with
Gods Will. However, when spiritual zeal is not tempered with
unconditional love and forgiveness, it is a fire which rages out of
control, as graphically demonstrated by the Inquisition. Therefore, if
we act with unconditional love and forgiveness at this time, we can
perform miracles once thought impossible, instead of burning alive
whatever does not agree with us.

Another possibility with this transit is that we attempt to rejuvenate
something, either an aspect of our life or something in it like a home.
There is a desire in us to restore and rebuild things now, on all
levels. But we must be aware that our energy and zeal may attract the
attention of others who oppose our beliefs. There is also a potential
within this transit that we will become obsessed with something. We
should stand up for what we believe is right but need to be certain that
we are speaking and acting from our deepest inner self, in other words,
we have to make sure that we truly believe what we say we believe, and
be ready to walk the talk, or problems will result.
